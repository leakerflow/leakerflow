However, we believe that the use of excerpts, comments, analyses, critiques, parodies, news, and other transformative materials related to these games may fall under the doctrine of "Fair Use" or similar exceptions and limitations to copyright in various jurisdictions, including Brazil (Copyright Law, Law No. 9.610/98) and the United States (Copyright Act, 17 U.S.C. § 107).

"Fair Use" is a legal concept that permits the limited use of copyrighted material without requiring permission from the rights holder, for purposes such as criticism, comment, news reporting, teaching, scholarship, or research. The determination of fair use is made on a case-by-case basis, considering factors such as:

*   **Purpose and Character of the Use:** Whether the use is of a commercial nature or for nonprofit educational purposes.
*   **Nature of the Copyrighted Work:** Whether the work is factual or creative.
*   **Amount and Substantiality of the Portion Used:** The amount of the original work used in relation to the copyrighted work as a whole.
*   **Effect of the Use Upon the Potential Market for or Value of the Copyrighted Work:** Whether the use negatively affects the copyright holder's ability to exploit their work or the market for it.

LeakerFlow encourages the responsible and transformative use of third-party content. However, we cannot guarantee that content generated by our users will always qualify as "Fair Use" or be exempt from copyright infringement. Users are solely responsible for the content they publish and must ensure they have the necessary rights or that their use falls within a legal exception.
